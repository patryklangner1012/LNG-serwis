After every intensive transport season comes a time when the workshop becomes the company's operational centre. Now — when freight activity eases off — our technical team carries out one of the most important phases of the year: comprehensive preparation of our own fleet for the next operational cycle.

## Downtime Is Work Time

For the workshop, there are no standstills. Periods of reduced transport activity are fully used for an in-depth inspection of tractor units and trailers. We go wherever daily schedules normally don't allow: hydraulic systems, LNG gas installations, suspension, braking systems, electrics and control unit diagnostics — every component is thoroughly verified.

## Scope of Seasonal Work

- Full electronic diagnostics of the drive units
- Inspection and servicing of LNG installations (seals, valves, sensors)
- Brake system and suspension check
- Technical condition assessment of trailers — frame, axles, installations
- Replacement of operating fluids in line with the maintenance schedule
- Road tests and parameter verification post-service

## Reliability as a Standard

A preventive — rather than reactive — approach is the cornerstone of our service philosophy. Leaving the season fully prepared means we can focus entirely on orders when the market picks up again. A fleet that leaves the workshop is ready for intensive work without unplanned downtime.

This is the foundation on which we build a reputation as a reliable company — not only for external customers, but above all within our own operations.
